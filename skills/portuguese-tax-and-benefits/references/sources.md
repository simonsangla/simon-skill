# Canonical sources — Portuguese tax and benefits

This file lists the authoritative sources to consult when verifying any factual claim before stating it. **Use them in the order given for each domain.** A blog or a tax-firm article may be clearer, but it is not authority.

---

## Order of authority

For every Portuguese tax / benefit question, the hierarchy is:

1. **Diário da República Eletrónico (DRE)** — the consolidated text of the law (Código do IRS, Código do IMI, CIMT, Código Contributivo, Decreto-Lei 220/2006, etc.) and the current Orçamento do Estado. Consolidated texts include amendments through their last update date — check that date.
2. **Portal das Finanças (AT)** — official tax administration. Carries the operational rules, Tabelas Práticas, Ofícios-Circulados (binding internal interpretations), Informações Vinculativas (rulings on specific taxpayer questions), and forms (modelos).
3. **Segurança Social — Guias Práticos** — the administrative interpretation that Seg Social applies day-to-day at counters and online. Often clearer than the diploma; legally subordinate to it.
4. **IEFP** — for active employment programs (ACPE, training, hiring incentives).
5. **Câmara municipal** — for IMI rate within statutory band, ARU certifications, local benefit deliberações.
6. **Court case-law** (Tribunal Constitucional, Supremo Tribunal Administrativo, CAAD arbitral decisions) — when AT's reading is contested.
7. **OECD / EU coordination instruments** — when cross-border (CDT, Regulation 883/2004 for social security coordination).

Sites listed below are the canonical entry points; deep links change frequently — start from the entry and search.

## Portal das Finanças (Autoridade Tributária)

Base: **https://www.portaldasfinancas.gov.pt/**

Useful entry points (verify the URL is current — AT reorganises):
- IRS section, including Tabelas Práticas (annual scale, retention tables): start from the IRS landing and look for "Tabelas de Retenção" + the current year.
- IRS Jovem: dedicated explanatory page on AT — search "IRS Jovem".
- IFICI: AT page under benefícios fiscais / regimes especiais.
- IMI, AIMI, IMT, Imposto do Selo: each has its own landing page under "Património / Imóveis".
- Códigos: AT carries non-consolidated PDFs of each Código; for the **consolidated** version use DRE instead.
- e-Fatura: separate portal — **https://faturas.portaldasfinancas.gov.pt/** — for invoice validation in the deduction workflow.

To find a binding interpretation: AT site → "Informações Vinculativas" → search by topic. These are written rulings issued to specific taxpayers but published, and they are the closest thing to a precedent AT will follow.

---

## Diário da República Eletrónico

Base: **https://dre.pt/**

- Consolidated texts (textos consolidados): `https://diariodarepublica.pt/dr/legislacao-consolidada` — search the diploma name (e.g., "Código do IRS", "Decreto-Lei 220/2006").
- Each consolidated text displays the **list of amendments** at the top and a "consolidado a" date. **Always check that date** before relying on the text — if amendments later than the displayed date are out there, the text may be stale.
- Diários (the official journal) themselves are at the base URL.

---

## Segurança Social

Base: **https://www.seg-social.pt/**

- Cidadão path → Benefícios → Desemprego (subsídio de desemprego, subsídio social, partial).
- Guias Práticos: each benefit and contribution scheme has a downloadable PDF guide updated periodically; check the publication date inside the PDF cover.
- Códigos: link out to DRE.
- **Segurança Social Direta** is the transactional portal (login required), at **https://www.seg-social.pt/inicio** → "Segurança Social Direta". For consultation of personal records and submission of declarations.

---

## IEFP

Base: **https://www.iefp.pt/**

- "Apoios e Incentivos" → catálogo de medidas (ACPE, hiring incentives, training).
- **IEFPonline** is the transactional portal (login required) for registration, convocatórias, and program applications.

---

## Câmara municipal

Each câmara has its own site. For IMI:
- Look for the **deliberação da Assembleia Municipal** fixing the IMI rate for the year — usually published in autumn for the following year.
- Some municípios publish their ARU map and benefit framework on the câmara site.

---

## Other useful sources

- **CAAD** (Centro de Arbitragem Administrativa) — tax arbitration decisions, at **https://caad.org.pt/**. Often the fastest place to find conflicting AT interpretations.
- **Portal da Justiça**, jurisprudência search for STA / Tribunal Constitucional decisions on tax.
- **ANAFRE / Associações Municipais** for municipal-level interpretation.

---

## Protocol for verifying a value before stating it

1. Identify what the value is keyed to: a calendar year, a contribution year, an OE diploma, an IAS multiple.
2. Find the controlling diploma in DRE consolidated.
3. Cross-check on AT or Seg Social for the operational application.
4. State the value, cite the URL, cite the publication or "consolidado a" date.
5. If your source is older than the most recent OE, **say so**. Recent OEs frequently amend brackets, ceilings, IRS Jovem schedule, IMT young-buyer regime — assume amendment until shown otherwise.

If you cannot verify a value within the current session, **describe the rule's structure without the value** and tell the user the URL to consult. That is strictly better than guessing.

---

## What NOT to use as primary authority

- Tax-firm blogs (Deloitte, EY, PwC, ECO, Idealista, Doutor Finanças, Comparamais, ComparaJá, etc.) — useful for orientation, not authority. Cite only if the official source is unavailable, and even then say "non-official summary".
- LLM training-data recall — discount entirely for any value-dependent claim.
- "What I've seen in past returns" — anecdotal.

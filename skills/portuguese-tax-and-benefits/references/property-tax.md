# Property taxation in Portugal

Three taxes on the *ownership* and *transfer* of immovable property, plus a stamp duty that overlaps with several of them. All three are administered by **Autoridade Tributária e Aduaneira (AT)**, but the assessable value (VPT — Valor Patrimonial Tributário) and the rates differ across them, and several rates are set by the **municipality** (câmara municipal), not the State.

> **Read this file when** the user mentions IMI, AIMI, IMT, Imposto do Selo (IS), VPT, prédio, primeira habitação, jovem comprador, reabilitação urbana, ARU, herança envolvendo imóveis, isenção predial, or any question about buying / selling / owning Portuguese real estate.

---

## 1. The base value — VPT (Valor Patrimonial Tributário)

Every urban property has a **VPT** registered in the *Caderneta Predial Urbana*, computed under the Código do IMI (CIMI), Article 38.º onwards. The formula combines a base value per m², a gross construction area, an affectation coefficient (use), location coefficient, quality / comfort coefficient, and an antiquity coefficient.

VPT is the assessable base for **IMI** and **AIMI**. **IMT** and **Imposto do Selo on transfers** use the higher of VPT and the declared transaction price.

Key handle: a property owner can request **avaliação geral** every 3 years from AT if they believe the VPT is out of date — frequently used to reduce IMI when the antiquity coefficient should have decayed.

## 2. IMI — Imposto Municipal sobre Imóveis

**Annual** tax on the ownership of urban or rural property as at 31 December of the prior year. Governed by the **Código do IMI (CIMI)**.

- **Rates**: set within statutory bands by each *câmara municipal* annually:
  - Urban properties — a band the municipality picks inside.
  - Rural properties — fixed rate.
  - "Devolutos" (vacant) and properties owned by entities resident in offshore jurisdictions — substantially higher penalty rates.
  Verify the exact bands and the current municipality's chosen rate on the AT IMI page and the câmara's deliberation.
- **Who pays**: the registered owner / usufructuary / surface-right holder on 31 December.
- **Payment schedule**: due in one to three instalments during the following year depending on the amount owed. Verify current thresholds and months on AT.
- **Family deduction**: an amount per dependent child living in the household, applied to the bill of the *habitação própria e permanente*. Confirm the current per-dependent amount each year.

### IMI exemptions
Several statutory exemptions; the most commonly asked about:
1. **Habitação própria e permanente, low-VPT**: temporary IMI exemption for the family's primary residence when the VPT and the household's taxable income are both below statutory thresholds. The exemption runs for a limited number of years. **2026 thresholds** — verify against **Art. 46.º EBF** consolidated on DRE (which also sets the exemption's duration) and the AT IMI page before relying on them: VPT ≤ **€125 000** for the property itself (the HPP-low-value test for the IMI exemption) and total household gross income ≤ **€153 300**. These are revised periodically.
2. **Urban rehabilitation**: properties inside **Áreas de Reabilitação Urbana (ARU)** that have undergone qualifying works can obtain IMI exemption for a fixed period after works completion, plus other benefits. Application is through the câmara municipal + AT.
3. **Permanent residence acquired by low-income households**: separate from (1), conditioned on household composition and income.
4. **Forest / nature-protected properties**, properties affected by natural disaster, properties classified as cultural heritage — case-specific.
5. **Properties of municipalities, religious entities, charitable institutions** — institutional exemptions.

Exemptions are **not automatic** — most must be requested explicitly via Portal das Finanças (Modelo 1 of IMI) within a deadline after the triggering event (acquisition, occupation as habitual residence, works completion).

### IMI familiar — per-dependent municipal deduction
Many *câmaras municipais* opt in to **IMI Familiar**: a fixed-euro deduction per dependent child applied to the IMI bill of the HPP. The standard schedule (where adopted): **€30 / €70 / €140** for 1 / 2 / 3+ dependents — the statutory per-dependent maxima under **Art. 112.º-A CIMI** (date not displayed; verify the current amounts against CIMI consolidated on DRE). Eligibility requires the agregado familiar to be correctly registered in Portal das Finanças by **15 February** of the year of payment. Adoption is at the câmara's discretion; verify on the câmara's deliberation for the year.

### IMI rate bands 2026
- **Urban**: municipal choice within **0,3% to 0,45%** (Art. 112.º CIMI).
- **Rural**: fixed at **0,8%**.
- **Devolutos / offshore-resident owners**: substantially higher penalty rates (verify Art. 112.º-B CIMI).
- For 2026, **31 municípios reduced** their IMI rate vs 2025 and **6 raised it**; the remaining 271 municípios kept the prior year's rate. Always pull the specific concelho's rate from the câmara's deliberação or the AT IMI page.

## 3. AIMI — Adicional ao IMI

**Annual** wealth-style surcharge on the *aggregate VPT* of residential and building-land property owned by the same taxpayer, introduced in 2017 (Article 135.º-A and following of CIMI).

- **Base**: sum of VPTs of habitação and terreno-para-construção properties owned on **1 January**. Properties affected by IMI exemption / non-incidence in the prior year are excluded from the base. **Commercial / industrial / services-affected property is excluded**.
- **Personal allowance (2026)**:
  - **Pessoas singulares e heranças indivisas**: deduct **€600 000** from the aggregated VPT.
  - **Casados / unidos de facto with joint AIMI filing**: deduct **€1 200 000** from the combined VPT.
  - The deduction is **per taxpayer**, not per property — owning shares across multiple properties still aggregates.
- **Rate structure (2026)** — applied to the *taxable VPT after the deduction*:
  | Bracket | Rate |
  |---|---|
  | First €1 000 000 above the deduction (single filer) | **0,7%** |
  | €1 000 000 to €2 000 000 above the deduction (single) — or €1 000 000 to €4 000 000 (joint) | **1,0%** |
  | Above €2 000 000 (single) or above €4 000 000 (joint) | **1,5%** |
  | **Entities (pessoas coletivas)** holding residential / building-land property | **0,4%** flat (separate computation) |
  | **Offshore-resident owners** | **7,5%** flat penalty rate |
- **Filing / payment**: AT issues the assessment automatically based on Caderneta Predial data at 1 January; payment due in **September** of the same year (single instalment).
- **Joint filing for couples**: opt-in via Portal das Finanças; default is separate. Joint filing doubles the deduction and applies the brackets at the joint thresholds — typically the better option above €600 000.

### AIMI interaction with IRS Categoria F
Residents holding rental property pay AIMI **in addition to** Categoria F income tax — the AIMI paid is **creditable / deductible** against the IRS coleta corresponding to rental income from properties subject to AIMI (Art. 135.º-I CIMI). The option is annual; choosing it forfeits the equivalent deduction in IRC for self-employed accounting purposes. **Verify Art. 135.º-I CIMI** on DRE before relying on this — the article has been amended.

Source: CIMI Art. 135.º-A e seguintes (DRE consolidado); AT página AIMI. Verify the rate brackets and deductions against the current consolidated CIMI before quoting.

## 4. IMT — Imposto Municipal sobre as Transmissões Onerosas de Imóveis

**One-off** transfer tax on the onerous acquisition of real estate (purchase, exchange with consideration, certain leases-with-promise-to-sell, share transfers where the entity is property-heavy under Article 2.º-A CIMT).

- **Base**: higher of declared price and VPT.
- **Rate structure**: progressive for urban property used for *habitação* (primary residence vs secondary residence have *different* tables, with the secondary-residence table generally heavier), single flat rate for non-habitational urban, single rate for rural, and a penalty rate for offshore-resident acquirers. Verify the current tables on AT IMT page — they are updated yearly with the OE.
- **Payment**: before the deed (escritura) — the notary will refuse to lavrar without proof of payment (Documento Único de Cobrança — DUC).
- **Refund / reduction** mechanisms exist where the acquisition is unwound, where the buyer subsequently qualifies for an exemption regime, or where a court decision adjusts the price.

### IMT exemptions / reduced regimes
1. **Habitação própria e permanente, low-value**: properties below a value ceiling for primary residence have historically been exempt. **2026 ceiling: €106 346** (4th-escalão limit, updated by OE 2026 with +2% adjustment vs €104 261 in 2025). Above the ceiling but below the next bracket, partial exemption applies.
2. **IMT Jovem ("jovem comprador") — first-home regime, Decreto-Lei n.º 48-D/2024**:
   - **Total IMT and Imposto do Selo exemption** for buyers ≤ **35 years** at the date of acquisition, purchasing their **first** habitação própria e permanente, with the property's value (the higher of price and VPT) not exceeding **€330 539** (2026 — updated from €324 058 in 2025; an OE 2026 +2% adjustment).
   - **Partial exemption** in the next bracket: between **€330 539 and €660 982** (2026), the excess over €330 539 is taxed at the IMT-jovem **8%** rate. Above €660 982: **no exemption**.
   - **Also exempts emolumentos de registo** of the first acquisition and the associated mortgage (up to the IMT-Jovem full-exemption ceiling, €330 539 in 2026), per DL 48-D/2024 — a frequently-missed additional saving.
   - **Eligibility conditions**:
     - Age ≤ 35 at the date of transmission.
     - **Not a dependent** for IRS purposes in the year of the transmission.
     - **No prior ownership** (full property, usufruct, or any parcelar direito) over urban residential property in the **three years** preceding the transmission.
     - Property must be **and remain** the buyer's HPP — **claw-back applies** if let, used as alojamento local, or otherwise diverted within the holding window (typically 6 years; verify the diploma).
   - **Joint purchases** (couples): if only one of the spouses meets the conditions, the exemption applies only to **that person's share** (typically 50%). Confirm the *quota-parte* calculation before quoting savings to the user.
3. **Urban rehabilitation (ARU)**: IMT exemption / reduction for acquisitions of properties in ARU intended for qualifying rehabilitation; conditioned on works being performed within a window.
4. **Funds, restructurings, agricultural land**: institutional or sector-specific regimes.

### IMT escalões 2026 (HPP — habitação própria permanente)
The HPP table runs across 6+1 brackets, progressive: rate 0% up to **€106 346**, then climbing through marginal rates to a single flat rate above the top bracket. **Secondary residence ("não-HPP") table is heavier** — 1% from the first euro, plus a lower top-bracket cap. Always pull the current table from **AT → Património / Imóveis → IMT**; the brackets are revised yearly with the OE.

### Useful taxpayer-side handles
- *Caderneta Predial Urbana / Rústica*, obtainable on Portal das Finanças, is the source of truth for the property's tax identity.
- Modelo 1 IMT is the declaration filed by the buyer (or notary) before the deed.

## 5. Imposto do Selo (IS) — stamp duty

A residual tax that hits many transactions a real-estate buyer or owner encounters:
- **Onerous transfers of immovable property**: a fixed-rate IS on the IMT base, **on top of IMT**. The standard rate has historically been 0.8% — verify on AT's Imposto do Selo page.
- **Gratuitous transfers** (succession, donation): IS substitutes for inheritance / gift tax in PT (which was abolished in 2003). Spouses, descendants, and ascendants are **exempt**; other beneficiaries pay 10% on the value transmitted (with surcharges in some cases for immovables). Verify exemptions on AT.
- **Mortgages, loans, suretyships**, rental contracts (initial rent or 6 months thereof, depending on duration), commercial leases — each has its own line in the *Tabela Geral do Imposto do Selo (TGIS)*.
- **Young-buyer / first-home regime** — IMT Jovem (DL 48-D/2024) **also exempts Imposto do Selo** on the onerous transfer (the 0,8% IS on the IMT base) **and** the registo emolumentos for the first acquisition + associated mortgage. The IS exemption on the **mortgage credit contract itself** (separate IS line) is **not** part of IMT Jovem — verify each line of the TGIS individually before quoting total savings.

For inheritance specifically: the *cabeça-de-casal* (estate administrator) files within a deadline after death (typically by end of the 3rd month after the deceased's death, with possibility to defer); IS is settled before the partilha is registered. Verify deadlines on AT's heranças page.

## 6. Mais-valias on real estate (interacts with IRS, not in this file)

The **gain** on selling a Portuguese property is taxed under **IRS Categoria G**, not under property taxes. See `references/irs.md` §4. Key handles repeated here for cross-reference:
- Residents: 50% of the gain is included in the IRS scale base.
- Rollover relief if the gain is reinvested in another primary residence within the statutory window — verify window and qualifying assets.
- Documenting the *valor de aquisição* (acquisition cost + IMT + IS + notary + works invoiced) is critical to reduce the gain.

---

## 7. Inheritance involving property

- IS at 10% on heirs other than spouse / descendants / ascendants (see §5).
- IMI continues to accrue on the property — the heirs (herança indivisa) are liable until partilha.
- Re-evaluation of VPT may be triggered.
- Tax-residency status of heirs matters if the property is sold post-inheritance (mais-valias rules).

## 9. Common interactions / pitfalls to flag

- **IMT Jovem** is conditioned on the property being and remaining the buyer's *habitação própria e permanente*. **Re-letting it (long-term lease or alojamento local) within the claw-back window** triggers retroactive IMT/IS at the standard rates. The claw-back window is typically **6 years** — verify against DL 48-D/2024 consolidated.
- **AIMI** is per-taxpayer, not per-property — owning shares across multiple properties can still aggregate. Couples should compute single vs joint AIMI before opting.
- A **municipality's IMI rate** within the band changes year to year; the user should not assume last year's bill repeats. Confirm with the câmara's deliberação.
- **Heritage and gift IS** has a separate filing path from IRS — easily missed by heirs handling the partilha.
- **Urban rehabilitation benefits** are conditioned on works qualifying — get the câmara's certification before betting on the exemption.
- **IMI Familiar** requires the agregado to be registered in Portal das Finanças **by 15 February** of the payment year — late registration loses the deduction for that year.
